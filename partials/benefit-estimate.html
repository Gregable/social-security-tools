Normal Retirement Age

Your primary insurance amount rounded down to the next lower whole dollar is ${{recipient.primaryInsuranceAmountFloored() | number:0}} / month and is the benefit you will earn if you retire at your normal retirement age (NRA). Normal Retirement Age is between 65 and 67, depending on when you were born.

For those born in {{birth.year}}, normal retirement age is {{recipient.normalRetirement.ageYears}} years and {{recipient.normalRetirement.ageMonths}} months. This puts your normal retirement date at {{recipient.normalRetirementDate.month}}, {{recipient.normalRetirementDate.year}}.

If you start collecting benefits then, your benefit will be the primary insurance amount, rounded down:

  • ${{recipient.primaryInsuranceAmountFloored() | number}} / month
  • ${{recipient.primaryInsuranceAmountFloored() * 12 | number}} / year

Early and Delayed Retirement

You can also begin taking benefits as early as 62 years old ({{recipient.dateAtAge(62, 0).month}} {{recipient.dateAtAge(62, 0).year}}), wait until as late as 70 years old ({{recipient.dateAtAge(70, 0).month}} {{recipient.dateAtAge(70, 0).year}}), or start any month in between. The longer you wait, the higher your actual benefit will be.

If you choose to take benefits earlier than normal retirement age ({{recipient.normalRetirementDate.month}} {{recipient.normalRetirementDate.year}}), your benefit amount will be permanently reduced per year you start early by:

  • 6.67% per year after {{recipient.normalRetirement.ageYears - 3}} years and {{recipient.normalRetirement.ageMonths}} months ( {{recipient.dateAtAge(recipient.normalRetirement.ageYears - 3, 0).month}} {{recipient.dateAtAge(recipient.normalRetirement.ageYears - 3, 0).year}} )
  • 5.00% per year before {{recipient.normalRetirement.ageYears - 3}} years and {{recipient.normalRetirement.ageMonths}} months ( {{recipient.dateAtAge(recipient.normalRetirement.ageYears - 3, 0).month}} {{recipient.dateAtAge(recipient.normalRetirement.ageYears - 3, 0).year}} )

If you choose to instead wait until later than normal retirement age ({{recipient.normalRetirementDate.month}} {{recipient.normalRetirementDate.year}}), your benefit amount will be permanently increased by {{recipient.delayIncreaseRate() * 100 | number: 2}}% per year that you delay.
